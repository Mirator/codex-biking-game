Ride the Horizon

Hold space to pedal. Use ← and → to lean between calm and swift moods. Pause and listen when the world calls.

🎨 Game Design Document

Project Title (Working): “Ride the Horizon”

Type: 2D Endless Exploration Game
Platform: Web (HTML5, WebGL, JavaScript/TypeScript)
Initial Development: ChatGPT Codex Prototype
Visual Style: Watercolor, painterly, surreal
Perspective: 2D side-scrolling

1. High Concept

Elevator Pitch: “Ride the Horizon” is a meditative watercolor exploration game where you endlessly ride a bike through living, hand-painted worlds that react to your pace, discoveries, and mood.

There’s no winning, losing, or scoring — only motion, discovery, and reflection. Every journey becomes a unique watercolor dream.

  • Genre: Endless Exploration / Ambient Adventure
  • Mood: Calm, introspective, magical realism
  • Platform Goal: Playable in-browser, responsive, lightweight

2. Vision & Player Fantasy

You are a traveler on an infinite watercolor journey — pedaling through shifting landscapes where color, sound, and emotion flow together.

The player should feel:

  • Calm from rhythm and motion
  • Wonder at each new world
  • Connection through memories left behind

3. Core Gameplay Loop

Loop Summary: Ride → Discover → Interact → Reflect → Continue

A minimal, meditative cycle that rewards curiosity and flow rather than skill or speed.

Stage Player Action Game Response Emotional Effect
Ride Pedal by holding a key or swiping; adjust pace. Terrain scrolls smoothly; watercolor effects react to motion. Calm immersion.
Discover Notice unique scenery, creature, or symbol. Visual/audio cues draw attention. Curiosity, wonder.
Interact Slow or stop to observe. Unlock a memory fragment or musical motif. Connection, reflection.
Reflect Ride onward; world subtly changes based on discovery. Color palette and music evolve. Continuity, emotion.
Continue Keep riding; transition to next biome. World remains seamless. Serenity, curiosity.
